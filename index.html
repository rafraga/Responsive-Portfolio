About Me

Mr. Fraga is an ATM analyst for ISA Software, assisting in the full life-cycle of analytical studies, ranging from date development, scenario validation techniques, metric generation and report writing.

Mr. Rafael Fraga has experience in air transportation and airport management, with a focus on simulation and modeling system performance and capacity analysis.

Mr. Fraga earned a Bachelor of Civil Aviation (2007) and a Master in Science Degree in Aeronautical Infrastructure Engineering (2009), with strong academic performance, publishing a MSc Thesis using simulation modeling with advanced techniques, including the RAMS Plus software.

During his studies, Mr. Fraga also obtained a private airplane pilot license (2007) and an aeronautical mechanic of engines license (2008).

Mr. Fraga performed several runway capacity studies for the McKinsey consulting company, in support of a large-scale Brazilian aeronautical project (2009).

Mr. Fraga has several published research papers on ATM system capacity, runway capacity, airspace design, new procedures aiming delays decrease; as well as economic research papers related to different aviation sectors.

The Brazilian Aeronautics Institute of Technology (ITA), a world-renowned institute of engineering, sent Mr. Fraga to present simulation modeling concepts in an international exchange promoted by AENA Spain in Barcelona, Spain (RPX - RAMS Plus Exchange, 2009).
